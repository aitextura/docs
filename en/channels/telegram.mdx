## Telegram bot

The fastest and free way to connect your AI employee to customers. You will need a bot token issued by @BotFather.

### Step 1. Create a bot in Telegram

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find @BotFather">
    Open Telegram and search for **@BotFather** — the official Telegram bot with a blue checkmark.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new bot">
    Press **/start**, then send the **/newbot** command.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the name and username">
    - **Name** — any display name (e.g., "Glamping Jawberry").
    - **Username** — one word in Latin characters, may contain `_`, must end with `bot` (e.g., `JawaBerry_Glamping_bot`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the token">
    BotFather will send a token like `123456789:ABCdefGhIJKlmNoPQRsTUVwxyZ`.\
    **Copy it entirely** — you will need it in the next step.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Do not share the token with third parties — it is the access key to your bot.
</Warning>

### Step 2. Connect the bot in AI Textura

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Channel section">
    Go to **AI Employees** → select the agent → **Channel** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Telegram">
    In the **Browse apps** tab, find **Telegram** and click on it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a connection">
    In the side panel, select the **Create** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the settings">
    - **Channel type**: Personal (one-on-one) or Group (for group chats).
    - **Platform**: already selected — Telegram.
    - **Channel name**: any name convenient for you.
    - **Bot token**: paste the token from BotFather.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect">
    Click **Connect**. The bot will appear in the **Connected** tab and start receiving messages.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you are connecting a group channel, make sure Privacy Mode is disabled in @BotFather — otherwise the bot will not see messages in the group.
</Tip>

### Step 3. Customize the bot appearance (recommended)

Go back to Telegram → @BotFather → **My Bots** → select your bot → **Edit Bot**:

- **Description** — a short description (up to 512 characters). Displayed when the bot is opened for the first time.
- **About** — a very short line, visible in the bot's profile.
- **Photo** — add a logo or photo for recognition.

## Personal Telegram account

If you want the AI employee to respond to customers on behalf of your **personal** Telegram account (rather than a separate bot), this is also possible.

<Warning>
  A **Telegram Premium** subscription is required to connect a personal account. The setup is done on the Telegram side, not in AI Textura.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect a Telegram bot">
    First, create and connect a bot using the instructions above — it is required for the system to work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Telegram business settings">
    On your phone, go to **Settings** → **Telegram for Business**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the chatbot">
    Select the **Chatbots** section and specify the bot connected to AI Textura.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the scope">
    Specify which chats the bot should respond to — **all messages** or with certain chats excluded.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After this, the bot will process messages, but the customer will see responses from your personal account.

---

## Bot vs Personal account

| | Telegram bot | Personal account |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **What the customer sees** | A separate bot with a username | Your personal profile |
| **Requires Premium** | No | Yes |
| **Setup** | AI Textura only | AI Textura + Telegram |
| **Group chats** | Supported | Not supported |
| **Recommended for** | Most tasks | Personal communication with clients |

## Voice messages

Telegram is the only channel where voice messages are supported. If a customer sends a voice message, the AI will:

- automatically transcribe it,
- respond with text,
- and can **voice the response** if text-to-speech is enabled in the employee settings.

In the employee settings, you can choose the voice, adjust speed, pitch, and pauses.
